History

Samut Songkhram Province or Mae Klong City In the past it was Bang Chang Subdistrict of Ratchaburi City. The name Bang Chang may be named after Chao Phlai (in 1630, Chao Phlai and Chao Saen, who were the first and second sons respectively of King Ekathotsarot (White), who was The only younger brother we know of of King Naresuan the Great (the black one) who had no sons. fled the royal danger and set up home in Bang Chang Subdistrict) Bang Chang Subdistrict has its center in Bang Chang Subdistrict, Amphawa District, Samut Songkhram Province. (According to the current administrative divisions) Bang Chang Subdistrict is also known as Suan Nok. (There is a saying that "garden in Bangkok, garden outside Bang Chang"). Later, at the end of the Ayutthaya period, continuing with the Thonburi period. Bang Chang Subdistrict is separated from Ratchaburi Province, called "Muang Mae Klong" Samut Songkhram has historical importance during the period when King Taksin the Great established Thonburi as the capital. The Burmese sent troops through to Bang Kung Subdistrict. King Taksin the Great gathered people and built a camp to defend the Burmese army until the enemy was defeated at the Bang Kung Camp area. It was considered an important protection against Burmese invasion into Thailand during that time.

The name Mae Klong city was changed to Samut Songkhram in which year it is not clear. But it is assumed that it changed around the year 1752 to the year 1756 because from the evidence in the Three Seal Laws concerning the Royal Decree on claiming compensation from wills. The name of the city of Mae Klong appeared. Samut Songkhram Province is a new land that was formed from the deposition of mud and sediment at the mouth of the river. It was born as an upland area until it became a large river basin.
